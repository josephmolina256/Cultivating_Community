Getting Started in Community Gardening

Why Community Gardening?

Community gardening fosters a sense of community, provides fresh and local produce, and promotes sustainable living. Engaging in communal gardening activities can also improve mental well-being and create a positive impact on the environment.

Supplies You Need:

  • Seeds or Seedlings
  • Soil and Compost
  • Gardening Tools (Shovel, Rake, etc.)
  • Watering Cans or Hoses
  • Garden Beds or Containers

Getting Local Approvals:

Before starting a community garden, it's essential to contact local authorities for approvals. This may involve getting permission from your local government or community leaders. Consider forming a committee or group to streamline the approval process and garner community support.
